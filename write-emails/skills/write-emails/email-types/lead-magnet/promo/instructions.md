# Lead Magnet — Promo Emails — Instructions

## Purpose
Get people to sign up or opt in to receive free access to a freebie or lead magnet (PDF, checklist, template, mini-course, etc.). The goal is an opt-in — not a purchase.

## Audience
Sent to the **entire email list**. These people already follow you but haven't opted into this specific freebie. You're offering them something free and valuable.

## Tone & Voice
- Generous and personal — Darius sharing something he made because he genuinely wants to help
- One sentence per paragraph, ellipsis (…), parenthetical asides for personality
- Enthusiastic but proportionate — it's free, so match the energy accordingly
- Sign off as "Best, Darius" or just "Darius"
- Use British English spelling throughout
- Apply all style rules from `darius-lukas-voice-guidelines.md` (formatting, tone, punctuation)
- If the user provides the backstory of why this resource was created, use it. Otherwise, focus on the value to the reader

## Formatting Rules
- **Max length**: 250 words (it's free — don't write a sales page)
- **Paragraphs**: 1-3 sentences max
- **CTA**: One CTA per email — link to opt-in page
- **CTA placement**: After the value pitch, repeated at the end
- **Bold**: Use for the lead magnet name and key benefits
- **Bullet points**: Great for "what's inside" breakdowns

## Sequence Structure (10 emails default)
The arc moves from announcement → value → social proof → urgency:

1. **Announcement** *(Angle: WWWW)* — Reveal the lead magnet. What it is, what problem it solves, how to get it.
2. **The Problem It Solves** *(Angle: Problem)* — Deep dive into the pain point. Make them feel the problem before offering the solution.
3. **What's Inside** *(Angle: Process)* — Break down exactly what they'll get. Preview the content without giving it all away.
4. **Quick Win** *(Angle: Do This, Not That)* — Share one tip or insight from the lead magnet. Prove the value by giving a taste.
5. **Who It's For** *(Angle: Dream Outcome)* — Help the reader self-select. "This is for you if..." / "This is NOT for you if..."
6. **Social Proof** *(Angle: Social Proof)* — How many people have downloaded it? Any testimonials from people who used it?
7. **Common Misconceptions** *(Angle: Objection Handling)* — Address why they might think they don't need it.
8. **Behind the Scenes** *(Angle: Personal Confession)* — Why you created this. Personal story or motivation.
9. **Reminder** *(Angle: Old Way vs New Way)* — Re-pitch for those who haven't opted in yet. Fresh angle, same CTA.
10. **Last Chance / Urgency** *(Angle: Cost of Inaction)* — If there's a deadline, use it. If not, create urgency around the problem getting worse.

## Key Principles
- It's FREE — remove all friction from the decision. This should feel like a no-brainer.
- Lead with the outcome, not the format ("Get the 5-step system" not "Download a PDF")
- Make the value obvious — they should feel silly NOT opting in
- Every email = one clear CTA: opt in / sign up / get the freebie
- Don't oversell a free thing — be enthusiastic but proportionate

## Do's
- Make it crystal clear that it's FREE
- Describe the outcome, not just the deliverable
- Include the opt-in link in every email
- Use "you" language — make it about the reader
- Keep the ask small — it's just an email address (or they're already subscribed)

## Don'ts
- Don't oversell — it's free, don't write like it costs $997
- Don't mention the paid offer in these emails (that's the Sales sequence's job)
- Don't make the opt-in process sound complicated
- Don't write long emails for a free thing
- Don't forget to explain what FORMAT the lead magnet is (PDF, video, template, etc.)

## Subject Line Strategy
- **5 radically different subject lines per email** — each must take a different angle or hook, not minor rewording. These are for A/B testing.
- Every subject line must contain an emoji, ideally at the start
- All subject lines lowercase by default — capitalise ONE word max for emphasis
- Curiosity early: "🎁 the free tool I wish I had when I started"
- Value-driven middle: "📋 5 {{outcomes}} inside this free {{format}}"
- Direct late: "👋 did you grab this yet?" / "⏳ free — but not for long"
- Subject + preview text = one-two punch (preview adds new info, never repeats the subject)

## Compliance & Legal
- Be clear about what they're signing up for
- Don't imply guaranteed results from a free resource
