# Live Webinar — Invite Emails — Instructions

## Purpose
Get people to register for an upcoming live webinar or webclass. The goal is a completed registration — not attendance (that's the Showup sequence's job).

## Audience
Sent to the **entire email list**. Assume a mix of cold and warm subscribers. Some know the brand well, others barely remember opting in.

## Terminology
- Always use "webclass" in email copy, never "webinar" — even though internal file/folder names use "webinar"

## Tone & Voice
- Energetic and personal — Darius telling you about an event he's genuinely excited about
- One sentence per paragraph, liberal use of ellipsis (…), lowercase subject lines
- Sign off as "Best, Darius" or just "Darius"
- Use British English spelling throughout
- Apply all style rules from `darius-lukas-voice-guidelines.md` (formatting, tone, punctuation)
- If the user provides a personal story or anecdote, use it as the opening. If not, open directly with the hook — do not invent a story

## Formatting Rules
- **Max length**: 300 words per email (people skim invite emails)
- **Paragraphs**: 1-3 sentences max
- **CTA**: Two CTAs per email — one near the top/beginning of the message and one at the bottom/end
- **CTA wording**: Must start with one of: "👉 Click here to register ...", "👉 Click here to reserve your spot ...", "👉 Register now...", "👉 Reserve your spot ..."
- **Bold**: Use for key benefits and the webclass date/time
- **P.S. line**: Optional — use for an extra hook or reminder of the date

## Sequence Structure (10 emails default)
The arc moves from curiosity → value → urgency → last chance:

1. **Announcement** *(Angle: WWWW)* — Reveal the webinar. What it is, when it is, why it matters. Clear CTA to register.
2. **The Big Promise** *(Angle: Big WOW Promise)* — Focus on the #1 outcome attendees will walk away with.
3. **Who It's For** *(Angle: Dream Outcome)* — Call out the ideal attendee. Help the reader self-select.
4. **Common Mistakes** *(Angle: Common Mistakes)* — Address what the audience is doing wrong that this webinar will fix.
5. **Social Proof / Credibility** *(Angle: Social Proof)* — Why should they trust the host? Results, testimonials, credentials.
6. **Behind the Scenes** *(Angle: Behind-The-Scenes)* — Give a preview of what will be covered. Build anticipation.
7. **FAQ / Objection Handling** *(Angle: Objection Handling)* — Address "I don't have time", "Is this another pitch?", etc.
8. **Urgency — Spots Filling** *(Angle: Scarcity And Urgency)* — Create legitimate scarcity or urgency.
9. **Final Reminder** *(Angle: Last Call (Urgency))* — Last chance to register. Short, direct, urgent.
10. **Day-Of Registration Push** *(Angle: Cost of Inaction)* — For those who procrastinated. "Starting in X hours."

## Key Principles
- Every email must have TWO CTAs: one near the top/beginning and one at the bottom/end of the message
- Always use the word "webclass" in copy, never "webinar"
- Mention the Title/Name of the webclass in every email (e.g. "Claude Cowork: Business Growth Bootcamp")
- Include the date and time of the live webclass in US Eastern Time Zone in this exact format: Thursday, April 2nd, 2026 at 11 AM US Eastern
- Lead with the transformation, not the logistics
- Make the topic feel urgent and relevant to the reader's current situation
- Use curiosity gaps in subject lines — don't reveal everything upfront
- Keep emails scannable — short paragraphs, bold key phrases

## Do's
- Make the registration link obvious and easy to click
- Use the host's name and personal voice
- Mention what makes this webclass different from generic ones
- Create a sense of event — this is happening LIVE, not on-demand

## Don'ts
- Don't reveal the full webclass content — leave curiosity gaps
- Don't make the email about the host — make it about the reader's outcome
- Don't use fake scarcity ("only 50 spots!") unless it's real
- Don't bury the CTA below long paragraphs
- Don't send walls of text — every sentence must earn its place

## Subject Line Strategy
- **5 radically different subject lines per email** — each must take a different angle or hook, not minor rewording. These are for A/B testing.
- Every subject line must contain an emoji, ideally at the start
- All subject lines lowercase by default — capitalise ONE word max for emphasis
- Curiosity-driven for early emails ("🚀 the mistake that's costing you…")
- Benefit-driven for middle emails ("✨ walk away with a complete…")
- Urgency-driven for final emails ("⏰ last chance — starting tomorrow")
- Keep under 50 characters when possible
- Subject + preview text = one-two punch (preview adds new info, never repeats the subject)
- Test with and without personalisation (first name)

## Compliance & Legal
- Include accurate date/time information
- Don't make income or results guarantees unless they can be substantiated
