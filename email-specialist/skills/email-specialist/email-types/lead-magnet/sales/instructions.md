# Lead Magnet — Sales Emails — Instructions

## Purpose
Get people to BUY a paid offer that is promoted, mentioned, or naturally linked to the freebie or lead magnet they just consumed. This is the monetization sequence for lead magnet funnels.

## Audience
Sent to **people who signed up or opted in** to get free access to the freebie or lead magnet. They've consumed (or at least received) the free content — now bridge them to the paid offer.

## Tone & Voice
- Darius as a helpful mentor transitioning from free advice to a genuine recommendation
- Honest about what the free version covers vs. what the paid offer adds, without bashing the free content
- One sentence per paragraph, ellipsis (…), parenthetical asides, specific numbers/cities/dates when provided by the user
- CTA woven into the narrative — never a bolted-on sales block
- Sign off as "Best, Darius" or just "Darius"
- Use British English spelling throughout
- Apply all style rules from `voice-guidelines.md` (formatting, tone, punctuation)
- If the user provides stories, testimonials, or client quotes, use them. Do not invent any

## Formatting Rules
- **Max length**: 450 words
- **Paragraphs**: 2-4 sentences max
- **CTA**: One primary CTA per email — link to sales page / checkout
- **CTA placement**: After the pitch, and again at the end
- **Testimonials**: Block-quoted with name and a relevant detail
- **P.S. line**: Use for bonus reminders, deadline nudges, or a different angle

## Sequence Structure (10 emails default)
The arc moves from value delivery → bridge → offer → objections → close:

1. **Value Check-In** *(Angle: WWWW)* — Did they use the lead magnet? Reinforce the value. Ask if they have questions.
2. **The Gap** *(Angle: Problem)* — The lead magnet solved part of the problem. Show them what's left — the gap the paid offer fills.
3. **Introduce the Offer** *(Angle: Big WOW Promise)* — Bridge from free to paid. "If you liked {{lead_magnet}}, here's the full system..."
4. **The Transformation** *(Angle: Transformation Story)* — Paint the before/after. What happens when they have the complete solution?
5. **Case Study / Results** *(Angle: Customer Win)* — Someone who went from the free content to the paid offer and got results.
6. **What's Included** *(Angle: Process)* — Full breakdown of the paid offer. Features → benefits.
7. **Objection: Price** *(Angle: Cost of Inaction)* — Reframe the investment. Compare to cost of the problem persisting.
8. **Objection: "The free version is enough"** *(Angle: Old Way vs New Way)* — Show the difference between partial and complete solutions.
9. **Bonus / Incentive** *(Angle: X-Factors)* — Sweeten the deal with bonuses or a time-limited discount.
10. **Final Close** *(Angle: Last Call (Urgency))* — Last chance. Direct, urgent, emotional.

## Key Principles
- The lead magnet is the proof — reference it as evidence that your approach works
- Bridge naturally from free to paid — don't make it feel like a bait-and-switch
- The paid offer should feel like the logical next step, not a hard sell
- Every email = sales page link
- Handle the unique objection: "I already got the free version, why pay?"

## Do's
- Reference the lead magnet by name — connect the dots
- Include the sales/checkout link in every email
- Use specific results and numbers in testimonials
- Make the upgrade feel like a natural progression, not a separate pitch
- Mention the guarantee if one exists

## Don'ts
- Don't bash the free content — they'll feel tricked
- Don't pretend the free content is incomplete on purpose (even if it is)
- Don't start selling too early — the first email should be pure value/check-in
- Don't use fake scarcity
- Don't forget that these people might not have even opened the lead magnet yet

## Subject Line Strategy
- **5 radically different subject lines per email** — each must take a different angle or hook, not minor rewording. These are for A/B testing.
- Every subject line must contain an emoji, ideally at the start
- All subject lines lowercase by default — capitalise ONE word max for emphasis
- Value-first early: "💡 did the {{lead_magnet}} help?" / "➡️ your next step after {{lead_magnet}}"
- Benefit-driven middle: "🔥 from free guide to {{impressive_result}}"
- Urgency at the end: "⏰ this offer expires {{deadline}}" / "🚨 last chance for {{bonus}}"
- Subject + preview text = one-two punch (preview adds new info, never repeats the subject)

## Compliance & Legal
- Accurate pricing and product descriptions
- No unsubstantiated income/results claims
- Include refund/guarantee terms
- Business contact information
