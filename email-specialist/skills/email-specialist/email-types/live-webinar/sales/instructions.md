# Live Webinar — Sales Emails — Instructions

## Purpose
Get people to BUY the paid offer that was promoted or mentioned during the live webinar/webclass. This sequence continues the sales conversation that started on the webinar.

## Audience
Sent to **people who registered** for the webinar/webclass (both attendees and no-shows). They've been exposed to the offer — now close the sale.

## Tone & Voice
- Confident and direct — Darius offering something he genuinely believes in, not begging for a sale
- One sentence per paragraph, ellipsis (…), parenthetical asides, specific numbers/cities/dates when provided by the user
- CTA embedded inside the narrative — never a bolted-on pitch block
- Sign off as "Best, Darius" or just "Darius"
- Use British English spelling throughout
- Apply all style rules from `voice-guidelines.md` (formatting, tone, punctuation)
- If the user provides stories, testimonials, or client quotes, use them. Do not invent any

## Formatting Rules
- **Max length**: 500 words (sales emails can be longer when telling stories)
- **Paragraphs**: 2-4 sentences max
- **CTA**: One primary CTA per email — link to sales page / checkout
- **CTA placement**: After the core argument, and again at the end
- **Testimonials**: Block-quoted, with first name and a relevant detail (role, result)
- **P.S. line**: Use for urgency reminders, bonus mentions, or a different angle

## Sequence Structure (10 emails default)
The arc moves from recap → value → objections → urgency → close:

1. **Replay + Offer Recap** *(Angle: WWWW)* — Send the replay link. Summarize the key insight from the webinar and re-introduce the offer.
2. **The Transformation** *(Angle: Transformation Story)* — Paint the before/after. What life looks like without vs. with the product/offer.
3. **Case Study / Success Story** *(Angle: Customer Win)* — Real results from someone who used the product. Specific numbers and outcomes.
4. **What's Included** *(Angle: Process)* — Break down exactly what they get. Features → benefits translation.
5. **Objection: "I can't afford it"** *(Angle: Cost of Inaction)* — Reframe the cost. Compare to the cost of NOT solving the problem.
6. **Objection: "Will this work for me?"** *(Angle: Social Proof)* — Address the "my situation is different" doubt with diverse testimonials.
7. **Objection: "I don't have time"** *(Angle: Objection Handling)* — Show how the product saves time or requires less time than they think.
8. **Bonus Stack / Added Value** *(Angle: X-Factors)* — Reveal or emphasize bonuses. Increase perceived value vs. price.
9. **Cart Closing Warning** *(Angle: Scarcity And Urgency)* — "Doors close in 24/48 hours." Create real deadline urgency.
10. **Final Close** *(Angle: Last Call (Urgency))* — Last email. Direct, emotional, urgent. "This is it."

## Key Principles
- Always reference the webinar — these emails are a continuation, not a cold pitch
- Lead with transformation and results, not features
- Handle ONE objection per email — don't cram multiple arguments
- Include the sales/checkout link in every email
- Use real testimonials and specific numbers whenever possible
- Create genuine urgency with a real deadline (cart close, bonus expiration, etc.)

## Do's
- Reference the webinar in every email (they share context)
- Use specific numbers in testimonials ("grew revenue 40%" not "grew revenue significantly")
- Address one objection thoroughly per email
- Remind them of the deadline in the last 3 emails
- Include a clear money-back guarantee mention if one exists

## Don'ts
- Don't bash people for not buying yet — respect their decision process
- Don't use manipulative tactics (fake countdown timers, false scarcity)
- Don't introduce new information that wasn't on the webinar — build on what they already heard
- Don't forget the no-shows — include a replay link in at least the first 2 emails
- Don't make promises the product can't deliver

## Subject Line Strategy
- **5 radically different subject lines per email** — each must take a different angle or hook, not minor rewording. These are for A/B testing.
- Every subject line must contain an emoji, ideally at the start
- All subject lines lowercase by default — capitalise ONE word max for emphasis
- Value-focused early: "🎬 what you missed on the webclass" / "▶️ the replay is ready"
- Benefit-driven in the middle: "🔥 how {{name}} went from {{before}} to {{after}}"
- Urgency-driven at the end: "⏰ closing tonight" / "🚨 last chance"
- Subject + preview text = one-two punch (preview adds new info, never repeats the subject)

## Compliance & Legal
- Include accurate pricing information
- Don't make unsubstantiated income/results claims
- Mention refund policy/guarantee terms accurately
- Include business contact information
